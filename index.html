Pasting
Privacy Review Guide Support

A native macOS clipboard manager that stays out of your way.

Built with SwiftUI. Keeps all clipboard history local, offline, and secure with Touch ID.

Download on the App Store

Features

  • Auto-Paste — Automatically inserts selected items back into your active applications using Accessibility APIs.
  • Types — Supports text, code blocks, links, files, and image copies (with responsive thumbnail scaling).
  • Custom Themes — Personalize with dynamic colors (Amber, Coral, Sunset, Crimson) and artistic background patterns.
  • Ignore Blacklist — Stop Pasting from recording copies inside sensitive applications (e.g. password managers).
  • Secure Lock — Lock your clipboard database behind Touch ID biometric verification.

Privacy Policy

Pasting works entirely locally. We do not run external servers, tracking systems, or database syncs. Your data never leaves your Mac.

  • Clipboard history is saved locally in your user profile container directory.
  • Transient and concealed fields from password managers are automatically ignored.
  • No diagnostic, clipboard, or analytics data is ever collected or transmitted.

App Store Reviewer Guide

Pasting requires Accessibility permissions to simulate Command-V keystrokes for automatic pasting. To verify:

  1. Open **System Settings -> Privacy & Security -> Accessibility**.
  2. Click **`+` (Plus)**, select **Pasting.app**, and toggle the switch **ON**.
  3. Relaunch the app, copy text, press `Cmd+Shift+V` to open the history, and click a row. It will automatically paste into your active text editor.

Note: If the switch does not toggle or has no effect, select **Pasting** in the list, click the **`-` (Minus)** button to remove it, then drag-and-drop the newly built `Pasting.app` file directly into the list and turn it ON.
